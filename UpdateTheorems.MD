# Update Plan for Theorems

This file contains the proposed updates for each of the 100 theorems found in `Theorems.MD`. Each entry includes a simplified one-sentence proof hint, a difficulty rating (0.0-10.0), and the relevant mathematics course.

---

### 1. Euclidean Algorithm
* **New Proof Hint:** The process relies on the fact that common divisors of (a,b) are preserved by the replacement of (a) with (a-bq), terminating when the remainder is zero.
* **Difficulty Rating:** 2.0
* **Mathematics Course:** Number Theory

### 2. Binomial Theorem
* **New Proof Hint:** Expanding the product of (n) binomial factors shows the coefficient of (x^{n-k}y^k) corresponds to the number of ways to choose (k) terms of (y).
* **Difficulty Rating:** 2.5
* **Mathematics Course:** Algebra / Combinatorics

### 3. Triangle Inequality
* **New Proof Hint:** The inequality follows from expanding the squared norm and applying the Cauchy–Schwarz inequality to the inner product term.
* **Difficulty Rating:** 3.0
* **Mathematics Course:** Linear Algebra / Analysis

### 4. Infinitude of Primes
* **New Proof Hint:** Assuming finitely many primes (p_1, \dots, p_n), the number (p_1 \dots p_n + 1) must be divisible by a prime not in the original list, contradicting the assumption.
* **Difficulty Rating:** 2.0
* **Mathematics Course:** Number Theory

### 5. Bézout’s Identity
* **New Proof Hint:** By reversing the steps of the Euclidean algorithm, the greatest common divisor can be expressed as a linear combination of the original integers.
* **Difficulty Rating:** 2.5
* **Mathematics Course:** Number Theory

### 6. Cauchy–Schwarz Inequality
* **New Proof Hint:** The inequality is derived from the requirement that the discriminant of the non-negative quadratic function (f(t) = |x - ty|^2) must be non-positive.
* **Difficulty Rating:** 3.5
* **Mathematics Course:** Linear Algebra / Analysis

### 7. AM-GM Inequality
* **New Proof Hint:** The inequality is proven by induction or by using the convexity of the exponential function, starting from the base case ((a-b)^2 \ge 0).
* **Difficulty Rating:** 3.0
* **Mathematics Course:** Algebra / Analysis

### 8. Fundamental Theorem of Arithmetic
* **New Proof Hint:** Existence is shown by induction, and uniqueness follows from Euclid's Lemma, which states that a prime dividing a product must divide one of the factors.
* **Difficulty Rating:** 3.0
* **Mathematics Course:** Number Theory

### 9. Prime Number Theorem for Arithmetic Progressions
* **New Proof Hint:** The distribution is established by showing the non-vanishing of (L)-series at (s=1) for non-principal Dirichlet characters.
* **Difficulty Rating:** 8.5
* **Mathematics Course:** Analytic Number Theory

### 10. Chinese Remainder Theorem
* **New Proof Hint:** A solution is constructed by linearly combining values that are congruent to (1) modulo one prime and (0) modulo the others, guaranteed by Bézout’s identity.
* **Difficulty Rating:** 3.5
* **Mathematics Course:** Number Theory / Abstract Algebra
